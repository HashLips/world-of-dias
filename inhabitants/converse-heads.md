# Converse Heads

## Overview

Converse Heads are reported Bloomline entities appearing as white heads with elongated necks rising from shared ground in clustered conversational fields.

## Description

Witnesses describe overlapping speech rhythms: gossip, jokes, arguments, confessions, and sudden chorus laughter. Individuals may lean or arc over others, creating a layered "canopy" of voices.

## Beliefs About Them

Some claim they are independent inhabitants. Others argue they are temporary manifestations formed by pooled social emotion in confluence zones.

## Narrative Role

They function as living atmosphere for scenes where social tone can shift civic outcomes before formal decisions begin.

## Notes

Observation confidence varies; most reports come from heavy-current periods.
