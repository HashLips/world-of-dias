# The Exhibition Resident

## Overview

The Exhibition Resident is a known gallery inhabitant type seen as a bodiless head-form that spends long intervals observing artworks in silence.

## Description

Residents are typically found at edge positions in corridors and corners where light falls across rotating panels. They rarely interact directly, but are consistently present through full exhibition cycles.

## Beliefs About Them

Some curators consider their presence a sign that a cycle has reached true depth of engagement. Others treat them as ordinary gallery locals with unusual physiology.

## Narrative Role

They provide a quiet witness presence in stories about art interpretation, curation disputes, and nonverbal civic participation.

## Notes

The artwork *Exhibition 1* is treated as the clearest portrait reference.
