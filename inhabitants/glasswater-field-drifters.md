# Glasswater Field Drifters

## Overview

Glasswater Field Drifters are inhabitants of southern Glasswater Fields who spend the night hours floating on the region's dam waters while watching the sky for signs of danger.

## Description

At dusk, drifters move onto the large clean-water dams and begin a long overnight drift on personal flotation tubes. The practice is both practical and ceremonial: each watcher keeps one tube for life, and many tubes are passed from parent to child as part of accepting the same role in adulthood.

Though the setting is quiet and beautiful, the work is considered essential. Drifters track the sky and stars through the hours when most settlements sleep, maintaining continuous visual vigilance until morning.

## Beliefs About Them

Across Glasswater Fields, they are widely respected as protectors whose patience and discipline keep communities safe, even when the exact nature of the threat is not discussed openly.

## Narrative Role

They embody one of Dias's core social patterns: calm rituals that preserve public safety while carrying memory of older sky-borne dangers.

## Notes

Local identity around the drifters is strong, but naming traditions for sub-groups and lineages remain only partially recorded.
