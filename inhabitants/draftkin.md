# Draftkin

## Overview

**Draftkin** are the most cited inhabitants of F960: process-native beings whose appearance stabilizes enough for recognition, but remains subtly revisable over time.

## Description

Draftkin bodies read like layered decisions. A typical observer notes a stable silhouette with visible line-memory: edge traces, faint construction geometry, and occasional "revision seams" where proportions refine across days or seasons.

They are not treated as unfinished people; in local terms they are **finished in motion**. Voice and gesture carry this quality as well, with pauses and redirections accepted as ordinary intelligence rather than uncertainty.

## Beliefs About Them

Outsiders sometimes mythologize Draftkin as prophecy beings or failed forms. F960 sources reject both simplifications: Draftkin are understood as normal citizens of a realm where persistence and revision coexist.

## Narrative Role

Draftkin embody a key Dias question: can identity remain truthful while remaining revisable?

Visual guidance: avoid horror deformation. Emphasize elegant, deliberate iteration.
