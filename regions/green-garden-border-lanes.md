# Green Garden Border Lanes

## Overview

Green Garden Border Lanes is a flower-walk landmark at the north-eastern Brindle March edge near the border with Glasswater Fields, known for thousands of potted green sunflower-like blooms.

## Description

From a distance, the site appears like a natural field of green flowers spreading across the borderland. Up close, visitors can see that every plant is individually potted and individually maintained.

The flowers are arranged in long walkable rows with narrow paths between them, allowing inhabitants to move through the display on foot. Many travelers heading east from Veloria City toward Glasswater Fields plan a stop here during holidays or off-time.

## Lore

The display is maintained year-round rather than wild-grown. This distinction is important in local identity: the lanes are treated as a deliberate civic-garden work rather than spontaneous nature.

Visitors often describe walking between the rows as a mental reset before entering a new annual cycle, and families returning to Glasswater Fields commonly treat the stop as part of the journey ritual.
