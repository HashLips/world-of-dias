# The Returning Span

## Overview

The Returning Span is the primary mappable region of F120, where recurrence patterns become visible enough to chart.

## Description

**Map portrayal guidance**  
Draw the region as a broad suspended arc-field with repeating route bands that rejoin at offset intervals. The core shape is a stretched crescent crossed by three semi-parallel travel lanes:

- **First Lane (outer)**: stable longest path; least variation.
- **Second Lane (middle)**: moderate variation; landmarks recur with altered details.
- **Third Lane (inner)**: highest drift; useful for quick passage but poor for certainty.

Use concentric broken rings, paired milestone symbols, and mirrored-but-shifted waypoint markers. Avoid strict grid realism; emphasize patterned near-repetition.

## Lore

Local charting uses "pass count" notation: first pass, second pass, and "difference marks" noting what changed between traversals.

## Stories or Depictions

Depictions often include paired bridge forms and twin towers with non-matching windows to signal "same shape, changed outcome."

## Notes

Do not render as temporal prison. The Returning Span is navigable variation, not a closed loop trap.
