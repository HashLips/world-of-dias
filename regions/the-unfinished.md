# The Unfinished

## Overview

**The Unfinished** is the primary mappable region within F960: a broad, traversable territory where land, structures, and routes hold long enough for charting, while still displaying visible states of incompletion.

## Description

**Cartographic portrayal (for map generation)**  
Map The Unfinished as a **layered draft-map** rather than one polished terrain pass. Use an elongated, asymmetric basin with five recurring sectors:

1. **Scaffold Arc (northwest)**  
   Step-like ridges and half-terraced ribs. Contours look intentionally interrupted before closure.

2. **Margin Flats (north)**  
   Pale chalk plains marked by alignment ticks, guide grids, and survey symbols that never fully erase.

3. **Revision Delta (center-east)**  
   Branching channels that split and rejoin in near-identical but never permanent courses; render like translucent overdrawn river strokes.

4. **Ghost Quarter (south-central)**  
   Repeating foundations and partial courtyards. Buildings appear 40-90% complete with exposed construction lines.

5. **Erasure Verge (southwest boundary)**  
   Soft edge where details blur, return, and shift by small increments; depict with gradient dashes, not hard borders.

**Color and texture direction**
- Base tones: warm graphite, chalk white, dusty slate.
- Accent marks: cobalt guide lines, copper trace marks, occasional ember corrections.
- Surface style: layered linework, visible underdrawing, revision ghosts, measured smudging.

**Scale language**
- Distances are serviceable but elastic: routes are charted in **passes** (first pass, clean pass, holding pass) as much as in miles.
- Landmarks are named by persistence (how long they hold shape), not by age.

## Lore

The Unfinished represents Dias-level becoming: a place where **incompletion is not failure** but active process. Travelers say it teaches a hard kindness: commit to form, but never worship finality.

Communities here optimize for adaptation. Architecture is modular, tools are multi-state, and social authority tends to favor maintainers, revisers, and mediators over conquerors.

## Stories or Depictions

The region is often depicted as a map "caught mid-edit." **The Half-Made Atlas** is the primary disputed artifact linked to this region's mutable cartography.

The **F960 Map** artwork is the primary current visualization of the region's sector layout (Scaffold Arc, Margin Flats, Revision Delta, Ghost Quarter, Erasure Verge) and pass-based scale language.

## Notes

For image generation, avoid ruined aesthetics. The Unfinished should look **deliberate and alive**: active drafting, not collapse.
