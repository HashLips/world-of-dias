# The Glassfold Ledge

## Overview

The Glassfold Ledge is F610's primary mapped zone, where reveal windows occur on predictable enough cadence to support field charting.

## Description

**Map portrayal guidance**  
Depict a stepped escarpment system composed of translucent ledges and folded reflective planes. The core map should include:

- **Primary Foldline**: the safest survey route during low reveal.
- **Three Window Shelves**: elevated sections where hidden under-structure becomes visible at intervals.
- **Blind Basins**: depressions that look simple until reveal windows expose complex sub-layout.

Use thin mirror contours, timing icons, and "visibility bands" marking likely reveal durations (short, medium, rare long). Keep the palette cool: glass gray, steel blue, pale white highlights.

## Lore

Navigation guilds time movement to "ledge minutes." Poor timing means traversing blind and missing structural cues.

## Stories or Depictions

Cartographers often print dual overlays: base-terrain and reveal-phase in separate line weights.

## Notes

Do not map as a puzzle gimmick. The ledge is functional terrain with timed information advantages.
