# F120 (frequency realm)

## Overview

**F120** is commonly described as the **echo band**: a frequency where events recur in recognizable patterns, but never repeat exactly.

## Description

F120 does not trap people in loops. Instead, it produces **near-return**: similar scenes, phrases, routes, and meetings with altered timing, tone, or outcome. Witnesses describe the realm as if memory and future were standing slightly out of sync.

In visual terms, F120 is represented through interrupted concentric structures, offset reflections, and trail marks that appear to have been walked more than once by different feet.

## Lore

The most cited internal region is **The Returning Span**, where recurrence patterns become cartographically legible enough for route planning.

### Awareness in F432

In F432, F120 is usually discussed in story circles and road folklore: "the same road, not the same day." Formal institutions treat it as plausible but low-confidence compared to F200/F500 documentation.

## Stories or Depictions

F120 often appears as ring motifs with slight breaks, doubled landmarks, and paired notes in ledgers.

The **F120 Map** artwork is the current visual reference for **The Returning Span** and lane-based echo geography; see `assets/f120-map.png`.

## Notes

Use F120 as thematic support for legacy arcs, repeated choices, and "history with drift," not as a hard time-loop mechanic.
