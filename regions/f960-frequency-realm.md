# F960 (frequency realm)

## Overview

**F960** is a high-variance frequency realm in Dias associated with **becoming rather than finished form**. People across other frequencies call it *the unfinished band*: not broken, not ruined, but persistently in-progress.

## Description

Unlike F200 (stable light) and F500 (void negation), F960 is defined by **partial manifestation**. Structures appear in successive passes: outline, volume, detail, then revision. Edges can sharpen and soften in the same hour. Surfaces often show under-sketch geometry as faint linework beneath their current form.

Color language in witness accounts tends toward graphite gray, chalk white, and muted mineral tones, with occasional sudden accents (copper, cobalt, ember orange) where a form is "trying itself out." Sound behaves similarly: hammer-like taps, distant scraping, and low harmonic hums with no obvious singular source.

## Lore

The best-charted region inside F960 is **The Unfinished**, a mappable place where the realm's incomplete logic is most legible to outsiders.

In comparative lore, F960 is treated as a conceptual counterpoint to both F200 and F500:
- F200 emphasizes coherent completion through light.
- F500 emphasizes erasure and non-hold.
- F960 emphasizes **iterative emergence**: form that is present, revisable, and alive.

### Awareness in F432

F432 does not treat F960 as a practical trade neighbor. Awareness arrives through sketchbook anomalies, recurring "almost-identical" diagrams, and a few disputed records (notably **The Half-Made Atlas**) that seem to redraw themselves between readings.

## Stories or Depictions

Frontier and artist circles in Nauw and Veloria describe F960 with workshop metaphors: "a world with wet paint that never fully dries." Scholars caution that this is a useful image, not a full model.

The **F960 Map** artwork is the current visual reference for realm-scale representation of iterative form and for the internal map logic of **The Unfinished**; see `assets/f960-map.png`.

## Notes

For visual interpretation, avoid polished finality. F960 should read as **intentional incompletion**: layered drafts, guide marks, ghost lines, and form mid-decision.
