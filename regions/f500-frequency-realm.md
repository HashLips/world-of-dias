# F500 (frequency realm)

## Overview

F500 is a **low- / inverse-resonance** frequency realm in Dias, widely called **the abyss frequency** or **the null band**. It is the conceptual and experiential **counterweight** to F200’s pure light: a domain associated with **absence, inversion, and the feeling of “nothing that still weighs on you.”**

## Description

**Cartographic / map portrayal (for visualization)**  
F500 is **not usually mapped as a country with towns and borders**. If it is placed on a chart at all, the honest depiction is an **indefinite field** or **void panel**: a region where the map stops being a picture of place and becomes a **warning shape**. Visually, artists often use **true black to charcoal fog**, with **no horizon line** or with a **horizon that appears to recede the longer you look**. Some charts show **only a dark oblong, circle, or torn hole** in the margin—an anti-map—sometimes labeled only with a number and a void symbol.

**Matter, light, and metaphor**  
Common speech compares F500 to **dark matter or antimatter** in tone: not always literal science, but a shared attempt to name **substance that annihilates normal contrast**. Light does not behave here as in F200; highlights feel **stolen**, colors **suck in**, and the eye finds **no rest points**. The environment reads as **anti-scenery**—a place where the mind reaches for a landmark and finds **a texture like forgetting**.

**Stability**  
There is said to be **no single “there” in F500** in the way other realms have a fixed there: distance is **unmoored**, and the sense of a named locale may be **a temporary coherence** in a body’s perception rather than a durable coordinate. Expeditions that return (rare) describe **pockets** or **falsely stable** intervals—brief intervals where a floor seems to exist—before the pocket **dissolves without transition**.

**Inhabitants (reputation only)**  
Stories acknowledge **whispers of inhabitants in the deep absence**, but **no culture agrees on proof**. These rumors are often grouped under the epithet **The Unraveled**—a name used in fear more than in taxonomy.

## Lore

F500 is the frequency most often used in moral cosmology as **“the opposite of Aurel’s peace”**—not a mirror image with tidy symmetry, but a **hungry null**: the idea that *something might still watch from nowhere*.

Diplomatic and scholarly records across F432 rarely treat F500 as a place to **visit**; it is a place to **name carefully**, map minimally, and avoid conflating with ordinary night or ordinary shadow.

### Awareness in F432

F432’s hold on F500 is **almost entirely indirect**: **tavern myths**, **sealed-ledger gossip**, **bleed-cold weather** that refuses a clean forecast, and **rumors of the Unraveled** that **no court will verify**. That leaves F500 **present in mood and language** while **absent from treaty tables**—a frequency you can **fear and name** without being able to **negotiate with it** the way you negotiate a neighbor region.

## Stories or Depictions

Frontier tavern lore claims that staring too long at an **empty corner** can “**tune**” a person toward F500, as if attention were a dial—another folk explanation for bad dreams and sudden cold rooms.

The **F500 Map** artwork is the current **visual reference** for void-field presentation, **F200** contrast, and **Unraveled** marginalia as **rumor**; see `assets/f500-map.png` and the artwork entry **F500 Map**. It is framed as **warning cartography**, not a guarantee of safe routes.

## Notes

For image generation, prefer **void-forward** compositions: **negative space, subtle grain, edge-darkening, lens-like distortion at the boundary, no friendly focal objects**. F500 should never read as a cozy gothic night; it should read as **erasure with teeth**.
