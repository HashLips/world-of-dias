# Mirror-Wake Inversion

## Overview

**Mirror-Wake Inversion** is a reported F610 interval event in which a person's reflected posture and affect diverge from their active body for a brief window.

## Description

During the event, the reflected figure may appear calmer, older, more wounded, or more resolute than the source body. Observers disagree on whether this is predictive signal, memory bleed, or perception error under reveal stress.

## Conditions

Most reports cluster around high-shear reflective zones in **The Glassfold Ledge**, especially after prolonged vigilance shifts and low-rest periods.

## Cultural Interpretations

Veil Scribes treat the event as admissible but low-certainty evidence and require phase-stamped corroboration before policy action.

## Notes

Often discussed with the artwork **Awake 1** as a visual shorthand for the inversion moment.
