# Green Garden Shift

## Name

Green Garden Shift

## Overview

Green Garden Shift is the recurring daily behavior of the potted border blooms whose sunflower-like heads and petals turn with light while shifting from lighter to darker greens.

## Description

As sun angle changes, the flowers gradually reorient in near-synchronized bands. At the same time, observers report a visible tonal migration in the petals: bright green hues under direct angle, then deeper greens as light moves off-axis.

The effect is most pronounced when viewed from the lane entrances, where thousands of pots create a moving color field.

## Conditions

Occurs consistently in the Green Garden Border Lanes under normal daylight cycles and remains observable through most clear and partly clouded conditions.

## Cultural Interpretations

Many inhabitants interpret the shift as calming and therapeutic. Travelers from Veloria City, especially those moving toward family visits in Glasswater Fields, often treat a walk through the shifting rows as a personal reset before continuing east.

## Notes

The flowers' precise botanical classification is unresolved in public records; current accounts emphasize observed behavior over taxonomy.
