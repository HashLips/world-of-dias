# Split-Tide Double Presence

## Name

Split-Tide Double Presence

## Overview

Split-Tide Double Presence is the observed condition in which Marek Solon appears as two synchronized presences in separate positions at once.

## Description

Witnesses report mirrored posture, staggered echo motion, and brief uncertainty about which presence is primary. The effect is most often observed during route decisions, ritual song intervals, and high-shift maritime conditions.

## Conditions

Most frequently reported in Nauw Outer Rim Seas under active tide movement, migration pressure, and ceremonial watch contexts.

## Cultural Interpretations

Some communities treat the phenomenon as inherited fracture evidence. Others interpret it as unknown biological anomaly, ritual amplification, or intentional performance.

## Notes

No accepted technical model currently explains the condition.
