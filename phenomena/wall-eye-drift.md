# Wall-Eye Drift

## Overview

Wall-Eye Drift is a reported Glassfold interval event in which repeated eye-like forms appear across broad wall surfaces and seem to track nearby movement.

## Description

Witnesses report synchronized gaze shifts without visible speech, signal, or mechanical source. The effect is strongest during medium-duration reveal windows and often produces route hesitation.

## Conditions

Most accounts occur near reflective wall planes under unstable light geometry, especially when travelers move in staggered single-file lines.

## Cultural Interpretations

Some interpret the event as neutral environmental feedback. Others read it as a warning that social intent and route discipline are being silently judged.

## Notes

Institutional use of Wall-Eye Drift claims remains cautious unless phase-stamped.
