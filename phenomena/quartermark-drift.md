# Quartermark Drift

## Overview

**Quartermark Drift** is a reported F120 timing effect where repeated events return close to expected schedule but offset by small, accumulating fractions.

## Description

Walkers describe the drift as "same bell, different edge": meetings recur within familiar windows yet arrive a little early, a little late, or with reordered sequence markers. Over long passages, tiny offsets can produce major route divergence.

## Conditions

Most frequently logged on multi-pass corridors in The Returning Span after several days of stable weather, when travelers become overconfident in rhythm.

## Cultural Interpretations

Refrain Walkers treat quartermark drift as a caution against procedural arrogance. The saying "count the fourth mark twice" appears in training notes and traveler etiquette.

## Notes

Often visualized with the artwork **Time Splits** when briefing outsiders on F120 timing behavior.
