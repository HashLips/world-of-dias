# Distant lumen-abyss bleed

## Name

Distant lumen-abyss bleed

## Overview

**Distant lumen-abyss bleed** is the collective label used in F432 for **low-intensity, inconsistent sensory residue** that people associate—by storytelling, instrument anomaly, and guesswork—with **conflict, proximity, or sheer scale** in neighboring frequency bands, especially the **F200** harmonic light field and the **F500** abyssal null. It is not proof of those realms’ inner life; it is **evidence that F432 sometimes notices something it cannot fully parse**.

## Description

Reported features vary by coast, city, and season, but **repeat just enough** to be named:

- A **“second dawn”** or **pale-gold stutter** along one strip of sky, as if a sunrise were **duplicated and one copy failed**
- A **pocket of cold** without wind shift, often described as **“absence with teeth”** (a folk phrase that traveled from harbor taverns to university halls unchanged)
- Brief **static in hearing**: bells damped, gulls late, a **pressure change** in the inner ear
- Rarely, **double-edged shadows** for a few minutes—objects casting **two almost parallel shims of darkness** at different depths

The phenomenon does not damage crops or walls on its own. It disturbs people because it feels **imported**—like a bruise on the world’s skin from a fight **elsewhere in Dias’s layering**.

## Conditions

More often reported in **open horizon contexts** (sea, long plains, high observatories) and during periods when **resonance instruments** (where they exist) show **nonsensical “mirror spikes”** not attributable to F432 weather or known Prime Bridge echoes.

## Cultural Interpretations

- **Folk / maritime reading:** a warning that **light and void still argue**, and the sea route you trust is only **one frequency thick**.
- **Scholarly reading:** a **threshold artifact** of incomplete models—F432 is **not closed**, only **stably forgetful** of what sits beside it.
- **Skeptical reading:** mass suggestion plus instrument calibration drift, salted by the **Partial Harmonic Index** rumor cycle.

## Notes

Treat bleed events as **story hooks and atmospheric canon**, not as direct invasion mechanics. F432 remains **largely insulated**; the phenomenon keeps **F200 and F500 present in world-feel** without requiring immediate plot impact on Veloria, Wabet, or Sorel.
