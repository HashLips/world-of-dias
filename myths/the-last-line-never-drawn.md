# The Last Line Never Drawn

## Summary

A core F960 myth says every maker who attempts to force a final, unquestionable line onto living form loses the work, while those who leave room for revision keep it alive.

## Myth Text

They tell of a master cartographer who swore to draw the one true border of The Unfinished and seal it forever. For seven days the lines held. On the eighth, the ink hardened, cracked, and lifted from the page like dry bark.

In the same season, a junior apprentice drew a weaker map with notes, alternatives, and empty margins. That map survived storms, arguments, and generations.

The elders say the lesson is simple: a last line can close a page, but it can also close a future.
