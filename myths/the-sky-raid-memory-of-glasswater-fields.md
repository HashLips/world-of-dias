# The Sky-Raid Memory of Glasswater Fields

## Summary

A longstanding Glasswater Fields myth says the region's night drifts began after repeated sky-borne raids in earlier ages, and that constant evening watch is the only reason those attacks no longer return.

## Myth Text

They say there was a time before the drifting lines, when people slept through first dark and woke to broken roofs, scattered grain, and claw-marks in water towers.

The raiders were never walkers from the roads. They came from above, winged and sudden, dropping out of cloud-shadow where no gate could stop them. By the time the bells rang, they were already gone.

So the fields changed their strategy. If danger comes from the sky, then eyes must remain in the sky.

Families carried tubes to the southern dams where water mirrors the heavens, and they took turns drifting through the night, reading stars, cloud breaks, and moving silhouettes. Parents taught children the routes, the signals, and the patience. Tubes passed down like heirlooms, not for comfort, but for duty.

Now when evening settles, the drifters go out and the villages sleep. Some call it tradition. Believers call it the watch that keeps the sky honest.

## Notes

The myth preserves memory of unnamed flying attackers; canonical creature classification is intentionally unresolved.
