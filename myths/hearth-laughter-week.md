# Hearth Laughter Week

## Summary

**Hearth Laughter Week** is a Hearthvale tradition—described in soft mythic tones even where it is real on the calendar—when **serious work scales back** and **games, songs, and silly competitions** take the town square. The point is not escape from labor forever, but the claim that **dignity includes laughter**, and a second life must have room to **shed weight aloud**.

## Myth Text

They say the first Laughter Week began when a joiner, a potter, and a baker bet who could make the **tallest structure from scraps** in one afternoon. The tower fell—of course it fell—and instead of anger, the crowd cheered, because the fall was **intentional and soft**.

Alwen is sometimes said to have watched from the side, not ordering anything, only **smiling** at the right moment so nobody felt ashamed to cheer.

In later years, the week gained rules gentle enough to be sweet: you may not mock someone’s first attempt at a new skill; you **must** cheer the worst singing; the last slice of pie goes to whoever **laughed the hardest** at their own mistake.

Sorel jokes that Hearthvale is naive. Hearthvale answers: **naïveté is a kind of tool**, like a hammer, if you use it to build a roof over grief.
