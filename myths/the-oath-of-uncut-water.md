# The Oath of Uncut Water

## Summary

A Wabet myth says Elyra bound the tribes by swearing no leader would "cut the river for profit."

## Myth Text

According to the tale, Elyra stood where three water paths met and asked each elder to place a hand in current before speaking.  
She then declared that any prosperity gained by wounding the water would return as famine in the next cycle.

Whether historical or ceremonial fiction, the phrase "uncut water" became a keeper standard in Wabet governance speech.

## Notes

Commonly invoked when rejecting outside extraction proposals.
