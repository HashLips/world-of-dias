# The Gallery That Finishes Itself

## Summary

A gallery myth claims certain works remain incomplete until they are watched long enough by silent residents.

## Myth Text

Curators deny literal paint changes in public statements, but night staff tell softer stories: that a line settles cleaner, a shade deepens, or a missing shape appears only after sustained, wordless attention. The Exhibition Residents are said to "lend completion" by staying where others pass too quickly.

## Notes

Retold during opening cycles to encourage slower viewing practice and reduce novelty-driven crowd churn.
