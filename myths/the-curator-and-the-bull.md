# The Curator and the Bull

## Summary

A gallery myth claims Zephan once staged a live contest with a bull to prove that art must carry real risk.

## Myth Text

The story says Zephan entered the floor unarmored, turning each movement into choreography rather than combat.  
By the end, neither fighter fell, and the audience left convinced they had witnessed both performance and trial.

Since then, "the curator and the bull" has meant beauty that refuses safety.

## Notes

Often retold during major exhibition openings at The Sketchy Gallery.
