# The Two Pillars Witness

## Summary

An Averra myth says that on certain ascension nights, two pale pillars rise above the crowd: one face of mercy, one face of refusal, and each watcher sees whichever face matches their hidden intent.

## Myth Text

Older tellings describe no thunder and no miracle fire, only still air and impossible height. The crowd gathers in silence. Two forms appear. One smiles without warmth. One frowns without anger.

Witnesses later disagree on what happened next, but all versions include the same result: private motives surface in public behavior. Those who came seeking spectacle leave quarrelsome. Those who came with repair in mind leave with difficult tasks already chosen.

The myth's warning is simple: ascension is not escape. It is being seen from above with nowhere left to hide your reason.

## Notes

Often used in Averra and Nauw discourse when debating leadership legitimacy and public ritual theater.
