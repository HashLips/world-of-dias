# The Drift Hour with No Captain

## Summary

A Driftfall founding myth (often told in dock jargon) describes the **first hour** the eastern territory was allowed to be itself: a stretch of time when **no one had rank**, and yet **a rule appeared anyway**—the line later **spoken, then written** into the territory’s ethos: *Freedom belongs to those who claim it*.

## Myth Text

They say, before the territory had a name, a storm stacked boats like toys along an unfinished shore. Nauw maps called the place “east Sorel hazard.” Sorel’s ledger called it "future Hearth overflow." The people who stayed did not have permits.

For one hour—some tellings say measured by a sandglass, others by a bell that never struck—**no captain, no magistrate, no exile-officer** could be found who would accept the chair. A fight almost began over who *would* refuse the chair first.

In that vacuum, a woman (or a man, or a pair—versions fight) called only **the First Claimant** said aloud: *If no one will steer, we will move anyway.* Crews shoved off not because a law told them, but because **the water answered**.

When the hour ended, Driftfall was not built; it was **declared in motion**, which is why Drift’s settlements still behave like they can pick up and leave, even when their houses look permanent.

Hearthvale critics call the myth a romance of irresponsibility. Drift residents say it is the opposite: it is a romance of **responsibility you cannot outsource**.

## Notes

Deliberately not tied to a single current leader; it situates "Free-Captain" models as *later codifications* of an older void-hour.
