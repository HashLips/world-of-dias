# The Glasswater Mirror Lull

## Summary

A Glasswater legend holds that the **great mirror dams** in the region’s south do more than store water: on certain still nights, they **store a hush**—a lull that passes outward along rivers until **Veloria’s bells** and **Brindle’s wheels** all agree to a softer tempo, because the **fields asked for** it.

## Myth Text

Farmers in Glasswater will tell you: bread has a body, and the body needs sleep, even if the world does not. When the **glass-bright** waters lie without ripple, the tale says, the **Mirror Lull** is active—an agreement between the dams and the sky-watch routines that the **soil is listening**.

Some versions go larger: the lull is a **rehearsal** for catastrophe, a practice stillness that trains the community to keep grain moving when a real threat arrives. Other versions are gentler: the lull is **gratitude** made atmospheric—when the water reflects the stars perfectly, the region remembers how much **depends** on a single unbroken season.

Nauw city dwellers, hearing this, either mock it or travel east to test it, coming home with half-smiles, claiming they felt nothing—and then speaking more quietly for a week.

## Notes

Ties the region’s **dam-sky watch** culture to **mythic world-scale** responsibility (food for Veloria, routes through Brindle) without duplicating the **Sky-Raid** myth; this is **tonal** and **seasonal**, not a raid narrative.
