# The Distant Companies

## Summary

A persistent but **unverified** F432 story claims that **organized parties** from the high light of **F200** and the abyssal null of **F500** have **clashed in border-space**—not inside F432’s landmass, but in **frequency margins** that ordinary maps and markets never quite draw. Most versions call these groups **“companies”** in the old sense: **bodies of people under charter**, supply lines, and banners only half visible to anyone tuned to ordinary soil.

## Myth Text

Sailors say the sea has a second tide you cannot time: a **pressure in the ear** and a **wrong color in the first arc of dawn**, as if morning arrived twice and the second one **lost a fight**. Cartographers in Veloria and dock prophets in the outer rim used to call that weather; younger chroniclers now sometimes link it to **distant lumen-abyss bleed** and whisper about **lumen companies**—lines of **gold-thread riders** and **haloed barges of light**—meeting **void-companies** described as **“companies of absence,”** which do not march so much as **unspool** until the horizon **refuses to hold a line**.

No witness in F432 claims to have watched a full battle. What people claim is the **after-echo**: cold that does not come from water, a **hush in gulls**, and the sense that a quarrel was settled **one frequency over**, leaving F432 to feel **a bruise in the air**.

Skeptics say it is a moral tale dressed as physics: a way to name fear of **unseen powers** without admitting ignorance. Believers say it is the true shape of history—**wars in the spectrum**—while the realm of harvests and ring-cities only catches **leaked noise**.

## Notes

The myth deliberately leaves **“companies”** ambiguous: it can be read as **military companies**, **exploration companies**, or **cult/charter bodies** without fixing canon. Future stories may tighten or contradict specific interpretations without breaking the rumor layer.
