# The Before-I-Kneel Oath

## Summary

A gate myth says the strongest wardens swear one line before dawn duty: *"Before I kneel to rank, I kneel to the law we promised the city."*

## Myth Text

Some versions place the line in Havren Pike's mouth; others claim it predates him by generations. The scene is consistent: a recruit is ordered to enforce a command that flatters power but violates protocol. The recruit refuses, expecting exile. Instead, an elder warden marks the refusal as the first true pass of gate service.

The tale does not celebrate rebellion for its own sake. Its lesson is narrower and harder: gate authority is legitimate only when it can refuse corruption even under pressure from higher voices.

## Notes

Often cited in DEB training disputes when officers argue whether a strict order is lawful or merely convenient.
