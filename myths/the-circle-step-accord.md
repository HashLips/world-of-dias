# The Circle Step Accord

## Summary

A Basin myth says the first stable truce between rival crews was achieved not by signatures, but by a shared stepping pattern that forced each side to move with the other's weight.

## Myth Text

Versions differ on names and decade, but the core image remains: two opponents entered a marked circle and were told to keep motion continuous until neither could force the other off rhythm. What began as a humiliation ritual became a revelation. Breath synchronized. Pace softened. Pride thinned.

When they finally spoke terms, neither side asked for total victory. They asked for workable recurrence: routes, rest, and repair obligations both could sustain.

The phrase "as one" later entered Basin speech for agreements tested in lived coordination, not only in legal wording.

## Notes

Modern civic circles cite this myth when training mixed-race crews for high-pressure mediation.
