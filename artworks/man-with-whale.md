# Man with Whale

## Overview

*Man with Whale* is an artwork by Daniel Eugene Botha that canonically frames the emotional interior of fisherman-whale contact: closeness, dependence, and latent danger.

## Description

The composition places a human body and whale form in intimate spatial proximity, using dense mark-making and high-contrast tones to suggest both tenderness and discomfort. The whale's visible eye and teeth reinforce the paradox at the center of the lore: the beings who feed civilization are also encountered as persons, rivals, and companions.

Asset reference: `assets/man-with-whale.png`.

## Real-World Role

A key interpretive image for the ocean-edge narratives of Nauw.

## Lore Connection

In-world, the piece aligns with accounts of Singing Fishermen who bond with some human-eyed whales while still participating in selective whale hunts.
