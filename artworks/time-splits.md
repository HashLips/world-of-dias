# Time Splits

## Overview

*Time Splits* is a clock-focused abstract showing displaced hands and nonstandard marks, often interpreted as a visual model of F120 near-return timing.

## Description

The face appears familiar at first glance, then fails ordinary reading: cardinal marks are incomplete, one index is replaced, and both hands seem to indicate incompatible moments at once. Warm and cool background folds reinforce the sense that parallel passes share space without fully merging.

Asset reference: `assets/time-splits.jpg`.

## Real-World Role

A visual reference for scenes where scheduling, memory, and recurrence drift just enough to alter outcome.

## Lore Connection

In-world, route scholars connect the image to **Quartermark Drift**, a timing irregularity tracked by Refrain Walkers when F120 passes repeat with slight offset.

## Notes

Commonly used in ledger-side diagrams to explain why "same hour" does not guarantee same event in F120.
