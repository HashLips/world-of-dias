# Balance 1

## Overview

*Balance 1* is a stark symbolic composition of a body arched under a beam with two red weights, often read as a portrait of governance under asymmetrical load.

## Description

The image uses high-contrast geometry and minimal color fields to stage physical strain as civic metaphor: a single figure bears the fulcrum while equal-looking weights still feel unstable. The posture implies control without comfort.

Asset reference: `assets/balance-1.jpg`.

## Real-World Role

A visual anchor for stories about leadership pressure, route logistics, and the cost of holding systems together in F432.

## Lore Connection

In-world, the piece is frequently associated with Brindle March debates over who carries transit risk for the rest of the realm, and is cited in discussions around the **Load-Balance Trial** tradition.

## Notes

Commonly displayed near administrative halls and route offices where burden-sharing policy is negotiated.
