# Fisherman Sing

## Overview

*Fisherman Sing* is an artwork by Daniel Eugene Botha used in canon to depict the Singing Fishermen and their close, unstable relationship with the whales of Nauw's outer waters.

## Description

The image presents a stylized human figure in dynamic contact with a whale-like form, emphasizing speed, bodily trust, and threat in a single frame. Its simplified, graphic language supports the lore idea that companionship and predation can coexist in one maritime practice.

Asset reference: `assets/fisherman-sing.jpg`.

## Real-World Role

A visual anchor for interpreting the Singing Fishermen motif in Daniel Eugene Botha's worldbuilding continuum.

## Lore Connection

In-world, the piece corresponds to fishermen who can both swim in friendship with known whales and hunt unbonded whales for survival supply.
