# Blue Bird

## Overview

*Blue Bird* portrays a massive avian form looming over a small dark-bodied figure, emphasizing asymmetry of size and vulnerability.

## Description

The creature's body is rendered as a dominant blue mass with an oversized green beak, while the nearby figure appears minimal and easily overpowered. The scene reads as a warning image rather than a naturalist study, echoing stories that one beak-grip is enough to take a person.

Asset reference: `assets/blue-bird.jpg`.

## Real-World Role

Used as a fear-folklore visual tied to child-safety storytelling and frontier caution.

## Lore Connection

In Hearthline oral culture, the Blue Bird image is often invoked by guardians as behavioral warning language: stay within marked routes, answer at call, and do not wander cold gaps alone.

## Notes

The artwork is treated as culturally true in effect, regardless of whether every telling is zoologically literal.
