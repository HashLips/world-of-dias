# Green Garden

## Overview

*Green Garden* is an artwork depicting the large potted-flower display on the north-eastern Brindle March border where thousands of green sunflower-like blooms face and follow shifting light.

## Description

The image emphasizes scale and order: rows upon rows of individually planted pots that collectively resemble a naturally grown field. Petals are represented in layered greens, from pale highlights to deep tones, reflecting the observed light-tracking shift.

Asset reference: `assets/green-garden.jpg`.

## Real-World Role

A primary visual anchor for one of Nauw's most visited border landmarks, used to communicate both the magnitude of the planting and the meditative experience of walking through it.

## Lore Connection

In-world, the piece is tied to holiday and off-time travel patterns from Veloria City toward Glasswater Fields, where many inhabitants intentionally stop to walk the lanes. The artwork is also read as documentation of the Green Flower Keeper's labor and the daily Green Garden Shift phenomenon.

## Notes

The title is commonly used as shorthand for both the artwork and the destination itself in travel conversation.
