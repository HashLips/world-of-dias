# And This Is

## Overview

*And This Is* is a stylized close portrait of a **zebra**, focused on the animal's eye and stripe geometry.

## Description

The composition crops tightly around the zebra's face, using bold black-and-white bands and a single watchful eye to emphasize alertness, pattern, and motion potential. The minimal framing turns stripe structure into the main visual language, making the animal feel iconic rather than purely naturalistic.

Asset reference: `assets/and-this-is.jpg`.

## Real-World Role

A visual reference for zebra-like wildlife in Wabet-adjacent story scenes, especially where observation, tracking, or quiet tension matters.

## Lore Connection

In-world, the work is commonly read as a field-artist rendering made after grassland-edge sightings near **Verdant Reach**, where striped runners are used in cautionary storytelling about distance judgment and herd movement.

## Notes

Frequently used in wildlife and travel-lore discussions as a shorthand for "do not mistake stillness for safety."
