# The Table Vase Warning

## Premise

A Basin rumor says that when a bright flower pot is brought indoors and the petals begin falling before dawn, the household is not cursed; it is being warned that someone inside is carrying unspoken strain.

## Story

In one commonly repeated version, a market family set a patterned street vase on the table after a successful week. By evening the flowers bowed. By midnight, three petals were on the wood. No storm came, no theft, no omen-light in the sky.

At dawn, an elder asked no prophecy questions at all. Instead she asked who had stopped sleeping, who had stopped eating with the others, and who had started saying "fine" too quickly. Before midday the house had shifted duties, reduced pressure, and reopened a conversation that should have happened sooner.

After that, neighbors began treating the table vase less as decoration and more as a social check: if blooms fail too fast, ask who is carrying too much.

## Notes

The story is usually told as practical Basin wisdom rather than supernatural doctrine. It aligns with F432 traditions that convert everyday objects into civic care signals.
