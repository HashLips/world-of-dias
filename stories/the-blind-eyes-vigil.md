# The Blind Eyes Vigil

## Premise

Averra watchers are said to keep a night protocol where witnesses sit in paired rotation and report only what both can confirm.

## Story

A common account describes a storm week when harbor rumors outran facts and mainland panic traders began doubling prices. The isle's watchers opened a blind-eyes vigil: two seats, one bell, one slate. No witness statement entered record unless mirrored by the paired observer before the bell interval closed.

By dawn, half the circulating claims had collapsed under cross-check. The remaining notes were plain, short, and unexpectedly calm: two lights, one ship, no landing, no fire.

The protocol spread beyond Averra as a phrase in Nauw clerks' speech - "blind eyes first" - meaning verify twice before amplifying fear.
