# The Third-Chair Breakfast

## Premise

A Verdant Reach custom says morning tables should be set with one more chair than confirmed guests.

## Story

One old telling begins after a season of boundary disputes. Two families who had stopped speaking still traded irrigation duty through intermediaries, each claiming they were done with the other forever. An elder kept setting three chairs for a two-person breakfast anyway.

For nine days the third chair stayed empty. On the tenth, the missing cousin arrived before sunrise, silent and exhausted, and sat without announcement. No speeches were made. Bread was passed. Work lists were updated. By noon the feud had not vanished, but it had changed shape from rupture to repair.

Since then, "leave the third chair" in Verdant Reach means: hold practical room for return before pride hardens into permanent loss.
