# The Night of the Wall of Eyes

## Premise

An escort team crossing the Ledge at reveal interval reports a full wall-tracking event and must decide whether to retreat, panic, or document.

## Story

The first watcher thought it was painted ornament.

Then the eyes shifted.

Rows of gold irises rolled in the same direction as the team moved, then stopped when they stopped. No mouths, no sound, no mechanism. Just attention.

"Keep lane. No running," said the lead scribe, though her voice had thinned to wire.

Two couriers wanted to bolt back downslope. One had already turned when the scribe called out timestamps, confidence levels, and line-of-sight references into her recorder. Naming the data steadied the group enough to keep formation.

At the final marker, the wall emptied. Purple stone. No eyes.

By dawn, three witnesses swore the event was warning. Two swore it was reflection error. The record was filed anyway, stamped and complete.

No policy changed that week.

But no one on that route mocked phase discipline again.
