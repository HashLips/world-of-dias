# Ledger mention of F200 and F500

## Premise

F432 did not **discover** F200 and F500 the way a survey discovers a cove. It **inherited hints**, then **named** them under pressure from **sensory oddities, traveler babble, and a few contested documents**. The **ledger mention** is the story’s compressed name for that process: a **sealed appendix**, a **harbor log marginalia**, a **ring-city filing**—sources conflict.

## Story

The version told most often in Nauw runs like this:

A **clerk or junior resonance-tuner** in Veloria’s older filing towers finds an entry that does not belong in the day’s work: a line appended to a **resonance-audit sheet**, in ink too crisp for the page’s age, listing **F200** and **F500** with two-word cautions. The sheet is resealed, copied badly, and leaked as gossip.

The version told on the quays is simpler:

A **sailor** came back **wrong in the ears**—weeks of hearing a **hiss where silence should be**—and sketched a **two-column diagram** in the margins of a **cargo manifest**. The numbers were not the sailor’s own hand; the sailor swore the sketch **arrived in sleep**. The manifest later becomes **Partial Harmonic Index** talk.

A third version, favored by those who like shame:

An **academic in good standing** mistook a **bleed day** for equipment failure, wrote a **formal error report** naming **“mirror harmonics at 200/500 (folk designation)”**, and thereby **accidentally** gave the realm clean labels for whispers that already had teeth.

Whichever version you believe, the outcome is the same: **F200 and F500 exist in F432’s language** before they exist in F432’s **competence**. The realm knows enough to be **afraid, curious, and half-right**.
