# The Load-Balance Trial

## Premise

A Brindle March civic practice says that when corridor policy deadlocks, the disputing parties must submit to a public "load-balance trial" before terms are ratified.

## Story

In one frequently repeated case, two guild blocs argued for six nights over whose crews would carry winter risk on the south span. Instead of another sealed-room vote, march wardens set a beam frame in the crossing court and required each side to name what burden they believed was "equal."

The declared loads looked symmetrical on paper. Under real route conditions, one side's burden collapsed in the first storm week while the other held with minor loss. The trial did not pick winners by drama; it forced policy to match weight as lived, not weight as claimed.

Since then, "balance trial" has become shorthand in Brindle governance for proving fairness under stress rather than announcing it in speeches.

## Notes
