# Whispers Before Signing

## Premise

A recurring Bloomline rumor says no treaty survives when signed while the whisper field is still active.

## Story

Two delegations arrived with terms already drafted and witnesses already chosen.

By the second hour, the Converse Heads had risen in a ring around the cove, repeating half-finished accusations and private worries neither side admitted in formal session. One envoy insisted on immediate signatures "before mood gets worse." The Tideheart recorder refused, citing hold protocol.

In the rumor version, they sign anyway.

By dawn the agreement had split into three competing interpretations, each claiming different intent from the same ink. Since then, people repeat one line whenever voices thicken before a contract:

"If the field is still whispering, the page is not ready."

## Notes

Commonly cited as cautionary oral precedent for enforcing pre-sign stabilization intervals.
