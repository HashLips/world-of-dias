# Ledger of the still march

## Premise

Velorian archivists and **Marchbound** scribes are said to keep a discontinuous file—**the still march ledger**—of **Brindle March stillness** episodes: timestamps, weather notes, and witness ranks. The file is not published in full; excerpts leak as **half-pages** in market rumor.

## Story

The most-circulated excerpt describes an afternoon when **Glasswater** windmills, visible from a mid-march overpass, **appeared to hold their blades** in the same few degrees of arc for longer than physics-lazy observers thought possible. Horses did not stop, people insist; only **sound** and **certainty** did.

A marginal note—signature scratched out—allegedly records **Bren Holloway** ordering a **charm re-tension** on three adjacent spans, not because they had failed, but because **"the old wards had gone threadbare in the hush."** Whether that order happened is disputed. Whether it helped is unknowable.

The ledger’s purpose, if the file exists, is not to *explain* stillness, but to prove **Nauw noticed**—that a realm built on **trade flow** can admit **flow interrupted** without collapsing into superstition unfiltered.

## Notes

**Status: unknown** keeps the ledger’s material existence optional. A future story can show the real book, a fake, or a **Prime echo** in the binding without breaking prior lines.
