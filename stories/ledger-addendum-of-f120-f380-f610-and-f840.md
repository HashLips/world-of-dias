# Ledger addendum of F120 F380 F610 and F840

## Premise

After the first rumor cycle around F200/F500, a quieter archival wave claimed a second ledger addendum naming four less dramatic but narratively significant bands: F120, F380, F610, and F840.

## Story

The "tower copy" version says a clerk in Veloria found a folded leaf tucked behind maintenance tables, labeled only as supplemental harmonics and marked for internal circulation.

The "quay copy" version says a navigator from Nauw Outer routes brought in a water-stiff page whose ink separated into four color families when dried by hearth heat, each line matching one of the four band descriptors.

A third version insists the addendum was intentionally downplayed by officials because it added complexity without immediate policy utility: useful for scholars and storytellers, inconvenient for budget committees.

Whatever the source, the effect in F432 was durable: those four bands entered language as **background truths with partial evidence**—enough to shape metaphor, ritual, and long-form story architecture without demanding full expedition programs.

**The F960 line (same rumor cycle, thinner evidence).** A fourth narrative strain notes that some leaves or shop copies of the addendum also carry a **fifth** entry—**F960**—or only a penciled margin gloss (*unfinished*), sometimes in a hand that does not match the four-band block. The line is **often absent**, sometimes **erased**, and where it exists it tracks **"iterative"** language familiar from **The Half-Made Atlas** talk: a band that behaves less like a fixed neighbor and more like a **map still negotiating its coastline**. F432 does not need to agree on whether F960 belongs on the *same* page as the four; the ledger’s job is to show that the **notation spread in waves**, and that **F200 / F500 / the four / F960** are all *named* in the archive gossip—each step less tidy than the one before.
