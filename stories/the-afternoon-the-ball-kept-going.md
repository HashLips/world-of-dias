# The afternoon the ball kept going

## Premise

A widely liked **basin** story—treated as true in spirit even when details differ—says that one slow afternoon, a boy and his dog at the field edge drew a **growing audience** without meaning to, until the whole patch of green became a game: **throw, run, return**, until strangers were passing the ball, parents were clapping, and someone brought **water in cups** like a festival nobody had scheduled.

## Story

Andrew and Russell are not always named. Sometimes it is a pair of sisters and a stick. Sometimes a retiree and a lopsided wagon wheel. The shared beat is the same: **the ball did not stop because anyone important arrived**; it stopped only when the **light** said so—golden, tired, kind.

Children remember it as the day the basin felt **round**—a circle of faces without rank. A merchant later swore he closed his stall an hour early and **earned more** the next day for goodwill alone (people argue whether that is economics or just **happier math**).

No one was hurt. No lesson was announced. A few songs started anyway.
