# Supplemental Harmonic Ledger Leaf

## Overview

The **Supplemental Harmonic Ledger Leaf** is a disputed archival page alleged to extend earlier frequency notations beyond **F200** and **F500** (first attested on the **Partial Harmonic Index fragment**), naming **F120, F380, F610, and F840** with brief cautionary glosses.

## Description

The leaf is reported as a single loose page with compressed column entries and marginal timing marks. Surviving transcriptions disagree on punctuation and one digit in the F610 line, but all major copies preserve the four-band cluster and rough descriptors (echo, heart, reveal, ember).

**F960 in the same paper trail**  
A subset of copies add a **fifth line** or a **verso margin** for **F960** (*unfinished / becoming*). Others omit it; a few show F960 penciled in a different hand and later **struck through**, as if a cataloguer lost nerve. The repo treats that line as **real enough to name** in Dias but **not settled enough** to treat as the same surety as the four-band block.

### In-world catalog sequence (how F432 orders the rumors)

1. **Partial Harmonic Index fragment** — first public hook: **F200**, **F500** (glosses: lumen; null / abyss).  
2. **This supplemental leaf** — second wave: **F120, F380, F610, F840** (background bands; story-useful, policy-light).  
3. **F960 line (disputed add-on)** — third wave, sparse evidence: *iterative emergence*, tied in lore to **The Half-Made Atlas**-style **mutable** cartography, not a third full expedition dossier.

## Cultural Meaning

For F432 scholars, the leaf marks the shift from "two dramatic neighboring bands" to a broader, richer **background spectrum** that can inform myths and story logic without immediate geopolitical consequence. The optional **F960** add-on is read as: *Dias has more bands than our budgets admit*, and *some of them are still drafting themselves.*

## Usage

Used as an anchor artifact for background references to the four bands in stories centered on F432.
