# Veloria ring-fair token

## Overview

**Veloria ring-fair tokens** are **small pressed-metal disks** (often brass or tin) issued at **outer-ring** fairs, harvest games, and guild-family days. They are **not** stable currency: they are **memory coins**—keepsakes stamped with a year-mark, a ring symbol, and sometimes a **joke motto** you had to be there to understand.

## Description

A typical token fits in a palm. The obverse might show a **simplified map** of the concentric city; the reverse, a fruit, a wheel, a fish, or a **smile-shaped flourish** with no official meaning. Children trade them, adults pin them in kitchens, and travelers carry one as **proof a happy hour happened**.

Vendors sometimes give tokens to anyone who **tries a game** and fails cheerfully, which is how the “losers' medal” became the **most** prized design of certain years.

## Cultural Meaning

In a city whose inner rings can feel **distant and severe**, the tokens are a **soft boast**: *I belonged to a crowd that was only trying to be happy together.*

## Usage

Lore: background texture for market scenes, fair chapters, and **uplift** B-plots. Real-world: optional prop for world art or merch continuity.

## Notes

Positive by design: **no debt**, **no weapon**, only **souvenir and belonging**.
