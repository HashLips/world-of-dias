# Partial Harmonic Index fragment

## Overview

The **Partial Harmonic Index fragment** is a **scrap of notation** (paper, vellum, or pressed laminate—accounts disagree) that surfaced in F432’s informal archives claiming to be part of a **longer index of post-Fracture frequency bands**. The legible section includes the marks **F200** and **F500** alongside brief glosses, enough to make **naming and numbering** of those bands **sticky in scholarly gossip** even when nothing else in the find can be confirmed.

## Description

When reproduced from memory, the fragment is said to show **column-like rows**: one line associating **F200** with **“high harmonic / lumen field”** language, and another associating **F500** with **“null / abyssal inversion.”** The edges are torn; there is no signature page, no official seal, and no consistent chain of custody. Several copies in Veloria and Nauw **do not perfectly match**—suggesting **copying error**, **deliberate fabrication**, or **unstable text** if the object ever sat near a bleed event.

**Material evidence in F432’s favor:** the fragment (or its story) is **plausible** next to other Prime-era notation habits: humans love **catalogs** even when the cosmos refuses to be complete.

## Cultural Meaning

For many, the fragment is the **“paper witness”** that made **F200 and F500** more than mariner’s poetry—at least as **labels** in debate. For others, it is **a hoax** that let scholars sound informed while still knowing nothing about **who lives in light** or **what names the void**.

## Usage

Lore use: a **concrete, optional anchor** for how F432 became **notation-aware** of neighboring frequencies while remaining **unaware of their full politics and geography**. Story use: a MacGuffin, a forgery trial, a translation puzzle.
