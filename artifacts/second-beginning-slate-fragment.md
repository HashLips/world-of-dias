# Second-Beginning Slate fragment

## Overview

**Second-Beginning Slate fragment** is the name for **surviving shards** of an early Hearth Compact charter—etched slate, sometimes re-fired ceramic—bearing **punched lines** and **repeated pledge phrases** from the first generations who treated west Sorel as a place to **rebuild** rather than merely survive.

## Description

Fragments vary in size; common marks include:

- a **row of notches** said to count mutual aid days after arrival  
- a **hollowed palm symbol** (interpreted as *open hand / no first strike*)  
- a **short clause** in plain language: *In the same drought, no one shall be **sent away twice** from the same well.*

Some pieces are displayed in **cooperative hallways**; others sit in private lockboxes, treated almost like **relics** of personhood. The **Kiln Fires of Second Beginning** myth is often referenced when people explain why fire-baked versions exist alongside **grey slate**—multiple generations re-making the same promise in materials they could trust.

## Cultural Meaning

The fragments function as **tangible "second beginning"**—proof that Hearthvale’s law is not only a sentiment but a **repeated, inscribed practice** of **mutual care**. Nauw scholars sometimes request rubbings, not to govern Sorel, but to study how **plain-language oaths of shelter** are written at a border where **pride and need meet**.

## Usage

Lore use: a portable token of Hearthvale identity; story use: a plot object in disputes over **land**, **kin**, or **who counts** as compact member.

## Notes

Compatible with but not required by **The Long Gate Argument**; the physical fragments anchor **Hearth** stories to **object permanence** while the bridge myth anchors **Nauw–Sorel** relation to **argument-as-infrastructure**.
